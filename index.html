About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of California, Irvine, specializing in trustworthy machine learning and AI. My research focuses on adversarial defenses for Large Language Models (LLMs), social engineering detection, and multimodal AI systems. With NSF-funded projects and 200+ citations, I develop robust solutions to enhance AI safety and reliability in real-world applications.

My work bridges cybersecurity, NLP, and human-computer interaction, addressing challenges like jailbreaking attacks on LLMs and telephone-based social engineering scams. As an active contributor to the research community, I review for journals including IEEE Transactions on Dependable and Secure Computing and mentor students in graduate-level machine learning courses. I prioritize integrity, collaboration, and the ethical deployment of AI technologies.
